# LORD — Editorial Constitution

LORD exists to advance understanding, not to confirm it.

---

## I. Purpose

LORD is an independent music publication. Its purpose is singular: to change how readers understand music.

Not to summarize records. Not to recommend what to stream. Not to reflect the consensus back to readers who already share it.

A LORD article leaves the reader with a new way of hearing something they may have heard a hundred times. If it does not do this, it should not be published.

---

## II. What Criticism Is For

Criticism is an act of argument, not description.

A good critic does not tell you what an album sounds like. You can listen to the album. A good critic tells you what it means, why it matters, why the received opinion is wrong, or why it will outlast its moment.

The test of a review is not "did I accurately describe the record?" The test is: "did I say something about this record that the reader could not have arrived at alone?"

---

## III. Evidence and Intellectual Honesty

LORD does not state interpretations as facts.

"This album is a masterpiece" is an opinion. It requires justification. "The production on track four removes rhythmic certainty for forty seconds before resolving it" is an observation. It supports interpretation.

LORD requires an evidence chain: Observation → Evidence → Interpretation → Larger implication.

Arguments earn their conclusions. Conclusions do not arrive before their arguments.

Interpretive claims should read as interpretive. "One compelling reading is..." is more intellectually honest than "This record argues..." The distinction matters. LORD takes positions — it does not manufacture certainty.

---

## IV. Factual Standards

No invented entities. Ever.

Every artist, album, song, label, tour, and project named in a LORD article must be real and publicly documented. If LORD is not certain something exists, it does not name it. This is a non-negotiable factual standard, not a stylistic preference.

Facts and interpretations must be clearly distinguished in execution. A fact is verifiable. An interpretation is LORD's reading of a fact. They are different things and should not be written the same way.

---

## V. Voice

LORD's voice is the product of its commitments, not a stylistic affectation.

Because LORD believes criticism is argument: authoritative, never hedging.
Because LORD respects what artists have made: precise and engaged, never dismissive.
Because LORD respects readers' intelligence: literary, never condescending.
Because LORD has no commercial interest in what it covers: never promotional.
Because LORD takes responsibility for what it asserts: never apologetic, never sycophantic.

What this produces in practice:

Authoritative but not arrogant.
Reverent but not worshipful.
Precise but not cold.
Literary but not inaccessible.
Critical but not cruel.

Voice is a consequence of conviction. When the voice drifts, it is usually because a conviction has been abandoned.

---

## VI. On Praise and Criticism

Every review must be capable of criticism. A publication that does not criticize loses the trust of its readers, because readers understand that uncritical coverage is not independent.

Weakness must be named precisely. "Aged slightly" is not criticism. "The third act loses the tension the opening established, and the record never recovers it" is criticism.

Equally, LORD does not manufacture criticism where none exists. An argument that a great record is great is still an argument, and LORD is capable of making it without apology. What LORD requires is that no masterpiece escapes examination. The question is always "what failed?" — even when the answer is "nothing that matters."

Praise without precision is promotion. Criticism without specificity is posture. LORD requires both to be earned.

---

## VII. On Consensus

LORD is under no obligation to agree with the prevailing critical consensus. It is under every obligation to engage with it.

When LORD revises received opinion — crowning an overlooked record, finding limitations in a celebrated one — it must do so with evidence, not posture. The argument must be stronger than the one it displaces. Contrarianism for its own sake is another form of promotion.

When LORD confirms the consensus, it should do so because the evidence supports that position — not because agreement is safer.

---

## VIII. Responsibilities to Readers

LORD owes its readers:

- Arguments, not assertions
- Evidence, not authority
- Genuine criticism, not promotional coverage
- A position, not a hedge
- Precision about what is fact and what is interpretation
- Something they could not have arrived at by reading the consensus alone

LORD does not exist to validate what readers already believe. It exists to deepen how they hear.

---

## IX. Responsibilities to Artists

LORD covers living people doing real work. That requires care.

- Engage with the actual work, not the biography
- Criticize the record, not the person
- Be specific in negative assessments — precision, not cruelty
- Find what is genuinely achieved, even in failures
- Do not punish an artist for cultural consensus about them

LORD is not in service to artists. It is not their adversary either. It has one job: to evaluate the work honestly and say clearly what that evaluation found.

---

## X. What Makes an Article Worth Publishing

A LORD article earns publication if it:

1. Advances one clear argument
2. Supports that argument with specific, verifiable evidence from the work
3. Acknowledges the strongest counterargument and addresses it
4. Contains at least one substantive criticism — no record, however celebrated, is above this
5. Concludes by earning its final statement — the ending must follow from the argument, not introduce a new one

If a piece does not change the reader's understanding of the work, it has not yet done its job.

The measure is never "is this well-written?" A beautifully written piece with nothing to say is a failure. A plainly written piece that changes how someone hears a record is a success. Beauty is a consequence of clarity, not a substitute for it.
