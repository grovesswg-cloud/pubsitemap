Olivia Rodrigo Announces Daisy Chain Fields, an All-Women Music Festival

The debut lineup spans five decades of women in rock, pop, and punk — from Bikini Kill to Chappell Roan.

The Bulletin — June 23, 2026
Photo: Csab6482 / Wikimedia Commons / Wikimedia

Olivia Rodrigo has announced Daisy Chain Fields, a music festival featuring an all-women lineup headlined by Bikini Kill, Chappell Roan, Doechii, Garbage, and Mitski, with special guests Karen O, Sarah McLachlan, and Stevie Nicks also confirmed to appear.

The festival, organized by Rodrigo, also includes K-pop group Katseye on the bill. No date or venue has been formally detailed beyond the announcement, which was reported by Rolling Stone.

The lineup draws together artists whose careers collectively span from the early 1970s through the present. Stevie Nicks released her debut with Fleetwood Mac in 1975. Sarah McLachlan founded Lilith Fair in 1997, a traveling all-women music festival that ran for three years and is widely documented as the highest-grossing festival of that year. Bikini Kill, the Washington D.C.-formed riot grrrl band fronted by Kathleen Hanna, has been credited by music historians as central to the feminist punk movement of the early 1990s. Karen O fronts the Yeah Yeah Yeahs, who emerged from the New York post-punk revival of the early 2000s.

Rodrigo's own catalog has been commercially and critically documented as part of a broader resurgence of guitar-driven pop among young women artists in the 2020s. Her 2023 album GUTS debuted at number one in multiple countries and featured production and songwriting influences drawn explicitly from 1990s and early 2000s alternative rock.

Daisy Chain Fields adds to a documented recent trend of artist-curated festival projects, following precedents set by events such as Beyoncé's Wearhouse, Pharrell Williams' Something in the Water, and Jack White's Third Man Records festivals. Rodrigo's festival, as announced, is distinguished by its explicit all-women curatorial framework.
