SWAVAY Discusses Metro Boomin Collaboration and Upcoming Tour

The Atlanta artist opens up about working with one of rap's most sought-after producers and the lessons he's carrying into his next chapter.

The Bulletin — June 23, 2026
Photo: Matthew Moloney / Unsplash

Atlanta rapper SWAVAY has spoken publicly about his experience working with producer Metro Boomin, detailing what he would have done differently during their collaboration, in a new interview with Billboard.

SWAVAY, who has operated largely outside mainstream visibility despite his Atlanta roots and proximity to some of the genre's most prominent figures, also cited James Blake as a significant influence in the conversation. The British singer and producer, known for his work at the intersection of electronic music, soul, and avant-garde pop, represents a notable reference point for an artist whose own work has drawn from melodic rap and R&B.

Metro Boomin, born Leland Wayne, has produced records for Future, Drake, Kendrick Lamar, and The Weeknd, among others, and is widely documented as one of the defining architects of Atlanta's trap sound over the past decade. His production credits include the 2022 album Heroes & Villains and collaborative projects with both Future and 21 Savage.

In the Billboard interview, SWAVAY also addressed his use of ChatGPT as a tool in his creative and professional process, describing it as an unexpected resource for career guidance. The disclosure reflects a broader, documented trend of artists and industry figures integrating AI tools into their workflows, a development that has generated significant discussion across the music industry in recent years.

SWAVAY is preparing for an upcoming tour, details of which were not fully disclosed at the time of the interview. The Billboard conversation marks one of the artist's more visible press moments to date, situating him within a wider cultural conversation about Atlanta's music ecosystem and the infrastructure behind its artists.

Source: Billboard
