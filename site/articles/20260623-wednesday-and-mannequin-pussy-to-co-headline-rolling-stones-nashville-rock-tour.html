Wednesday and Mannequin Pussy to Co-Headline Rolling Stone's Nashville Rock Tour

Two of independent rock's most critically recognized acts will share a stage at Nashville's The Truth on November 2.

The Bulletin — June 23, 2026
Photo: Wikipedia — Wednesday (American band) / Wikipedia

Wednesday and Mannequin Pussy are set to co-headline a Rolling Stone-presented rock tour stop in Nashville, Tennessee, performing together at The Truth on November 2.

The show is part of Rolling Stone's Rock Tour, a live event series organized under the publication's brand. Rolling Stone, founded in San Francisco in 1967, has operated as one of the longest-running music and culture magazines in the United States and has expanded its presence into live events in recent years.

Wednesday is a rock band from Asheville, North Carolina, led by vocalist and guitarist Karly Hartzman. The band released Rat Saw God in 2023, a record that drew significant critical attention and placed the group among a renewed wave of interest in American indie and alt-country inflected rock. Mannequin Pussy, a Philadelphia-based band fronted by Marisa Dabice, released I Got Heaven in 2024, continuing a trajectory that has seen the band move from underground punk circuits to wider critical recognition.

Nashville, historically associated with country music infrastructure, has also maintained an active independent rock venue ecosystem. The Truth is among the city's established live music spaces.

The co-headlining structure places both acts on equal billing, a format that documents a parity in their current standing within the independent rock landscape as recognized by the presenting publication.
