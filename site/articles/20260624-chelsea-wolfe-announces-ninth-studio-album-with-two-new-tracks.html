Chelsea Wolfe Announces Ninth Studio Album With Two New Tracks

The California artist surfaces from silence with 'The Dark' and 'Death Is Not The End,' alongside a transatlantic tour.

The Bulletin — June 24, 2026
Photo: Allan Wan / Wikimedia Commons / Wikimedia

Chelsea Wolfe has released two new songs, 'The Dark' and 'Death Is Not The End,' marking the first material from her forthcoming ninth studio album. The announcement arrives alongside confirmed tour dates spanning the United States and Europe.

Wolfe has been a sustained presence in the intersection of doom folk, dark ambient, and art rock since her self-released debut, The Grime and the Glow, in 2010. Her catalog across labels has included Apokalypsis (2011), Pain Is Beauty (2013), Abyss (2015), Hiss Spun (2017), and Birth of Violence (2019), the last of which stripped her arrangements to their most austere acoustic form. A ninth studio record would represent her first full-length release since that 2019 effort.

The two tracks — their titles, 'The Dark' and 'Death Is Not The End,' consistent with recurring thematic preoccupations throughout her body of work — serve as the formal introduction to the new album cycle. No album title or release date has been announced at this time.

The accompanying tour represents Wolfe's commitment to both North American and European markets, continuing a pattern of international touring that has defined her live presence since the early 2010s. Specific dates and venues have been made available via NME's original report.

With two songs now on record and a tour confirmed, Wolfe's ninth studio album enters the documented timeline of her catalogue.
