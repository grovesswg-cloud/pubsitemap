The Strokes Cover The Walkmen's 'Heaven' With Hamilton Leithauser in Boston

Two pillars of New York indie rock shared a stage to perform one of the movement's most enduring songs.

The Bulletin — June 24, 2026
Photo: Wikipedia — The Strokes / Wikipedia

During a recent live performance in Boston, The Strokes invited Hamilton Leithauser onstage to perform a cover of The Walkmen's 2012 song 'Heaven,' the title track from that band's final studio album.

Leithauser served as the lead vocalist of The Walkmen from the band's formation in Washington, D.C. in 2000 through their indefinite hiatus in 2013. 'Heaven,' released on Fat Possum Records, was widely received as one of the defining records of its year and stands as The Walkmen's farewell document. The song itself is a centerpiece of that album — spare, hymnal, and built around Leithauser's characteristically strained tenor.

The Strokes and The Walkmen emerged from overlapping scenes in the early 2000s, both groups carrying the banner of a New York rock revival that reshaped independent music at the turn of the millennium. The Strokes debuted with Is This It in 2001; The Walkmen followed shortly after with Everyone Who Pretended to Like Me Is Gone in 2002. The two bands have long been linked by geography, era, and critical history, though formal collaborations between their members have been rare.

Leithauser has remained active since The Walkmen's hiatus, releasing solo material and collaborating with Rostam Batmanglij of Vampire Weekend on the 2016 record I Had a Dream That You Were Mine. The Walkmen reunited for live performances in 2022 and 2023 before Leithauser publicly confirmed in 2024 that the reunion had concluded.

The Boston performance adds a documented live cover of 'Heaven' to The Strokes' concert history and marks a recorded instance of Leithauser performing the song outside the context of The Walkmen.
