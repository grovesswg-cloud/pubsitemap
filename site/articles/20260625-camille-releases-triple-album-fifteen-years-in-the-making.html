Camille Releases Triple Album Fifteen Years in the Making

The Sound of Milk documents birth, childhood, and adolescence across three records — and a decade and a half of deliberate silence.

The Bulletin — June 25, 2026
Photo: Wikipedia — Camille (French singer) / Wikipedia

French singer Camille has released The Sound of Milk, a triple album fifteen years in the making, documenting her experience raising two children with composer Clément Ducol.

The record is structured in three parts, each corresponding to a distinct stage of childhood. Naissance, the first, was completed in 2015. Enfance followed in 2020. Adolescence arrives in 2025. Camille held all three parts from release until now, telling The Guardian she was not ready to speak about the work while her children were still young. Her son and daughter are now teenagers.

Camille — born Camille Dalmais — is known for an unconventional approach to the voice and body as instrument, a practice that brought her widespread critical recognition in France and internationally. She won an Academy Award for Best Original Song alongside Ducol for "What Was I Made For?", written for the 2023 film Barbie. Ducol, her partner and collaborator, is the composer of the music underpinning The Sound of Milk.

The decision to withhold each completed installment rather than release it upon completion is a documented part of the project's construction. Camille has stated that the material's intimacy — its engagement with beauty, joy, and the psychological depth of parenthood — required distance before public exposure was possible.

The Sound of Milk is among the longest-gestating projects in Camille's catalog, which includes the albums Le Fil (2005), Music Hole (2008), Ilo Veyou (2011), and OUI (2019). The release marks her first full-length work since OUI and her first major project to address motherhood and domestic life as its primary subject.
