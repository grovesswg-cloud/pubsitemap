Olivia Rodrigo's Daisy Chain Fields Festival Sells Out in Thirty Minutes

The pop star's inaugural all-women festival moves from announcement to sellout before most fans reached the checkout.

The Bulletin — June 25, 2026
Photo: Wikipedia — Olivia Rodrigo / Wikipedia

Olivia Rodrigo's debut music festival, Daisy Chain Fields, sold out within thirty minutes of tickets going on sale, according to reporting by Rolling Stone.

The festival's lineup is composed entirely of women, featuring Stevie Nicks, Mitski, Bikini Kill, and Doechii alongside Rodrigo herself. The booking spans multiple generations and genres of music made by women: Nicks has been a recording and touring artist since the early 1970s, first with Fleetwood Mac and later as a solo act; Bikini Kill were central figures in the riot grrrl movement of the early 1990s; Mitski has been releasing critically recognized independent and major-label records since 2012; and Doechii earned a Grammy Award for Best Rap Album at the 2025 ceremony for her mixtape Alligator Bites Never Heal.

Rodrigo, who released her debut album SOUR in 2021 and her second album GUTS in 2023, has not previously produced or headlined a festival of this kind. Daisy Chain Fields marks her first venture into festival curation and production.

The sellout timeline places Daisy Chain Fields alongside a documented pattern of demand for Rodrigo's live events. Her GUTS World Tour, which ran through 2024, sold out arenas across North America, Europe, and Australia.

The all-women curatorial concept is the stated organizing principle of the festival's lineup, as reported. The booking of an artist of Stevie Nicks's catalog stature alongside newer acts such as Doechii places the festival on record as spanning more than five decades of women in popular music.
