Phoebe Bridgers Returns With New Album Lost Boys on Dead Oceans

The singer-songwriter's first solo record since 2020's Punisher arrives after a public withdrawal following Boygenius's hiatus.

The Bulletin — June 26, 2026
Photo: Wikipedia — Phoebe Bridgers / Wikipedia

Phoebe Bridgers has released Lost Boys, her third solo studio album, on Dead Oceans. The record marks her first solo release since Punisher in 2020 and follows a deliberate step back from public life that began when Boygenius — the supergroup she formed with Julien Baker and Lucy Dacus — announced a hiatus in February 2024.

Punisher, released in June 2020, became a commercial and critical landmark, its release coinciding with pandemic lockdowns and reaching audiences well beyond the independent rock world in which Bridgers had built her reputation. The album's success brought with it a level of fan attention that became, by documented accounts, invasive in character.

Boygenius formed in 2018, releasing a self-titled EP before the three members pursued separate projects. The supergroup's debut full-length, The Record, arrived in March 2023 on Interscope Records to widespread critical recognition, extending the visibility of all three artists before the group's pause the following year.

Lost Boys is described in reviews as an ornate reinvention, continuing Bridgers's association with introspective indie-rock while marking a shift from the sonic territory of her previous solo work. The album is out now on Dead Oceans, the Bloomington, Indiana-based independent label that has released all of Bridgers's solo records.
