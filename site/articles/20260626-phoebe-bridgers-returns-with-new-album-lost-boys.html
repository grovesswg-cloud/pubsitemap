Phoebe Bridgers Returns With New Album Lost Boys

The singer-songwriter's first solo record since 2020's Punisher arrives on Dead Oceans after a deliberate withdrawal from public life.

The Bulletin — June 26, 2026
Photo: Wikipedia — Phoebe Bridgers / Wikipedia

Phoebe Bridgers has released Lost Boys, her third solo studio album, on Dead Oceans. The record marks her first solo full-length since Punisher in June 2020.

Punisher, Bridgers' second album, was released during the early months of the COVID-19 pandemic and became a commercial and critical breakout, earning her four Grammy nominations in 2021 across categories including Best New Artist and Best Rock Performance. Its success transformed Bridgers from a respected independent artist into a figure of mainstream pop-cultural prominence.

Following that profile expansion, Bridgers co-founded Boygenius, the supergroup with Julien Baker and Lucy Dacus. The trio released their debut full-length, The Record, in March 2023 on Interscope, which won three Grammy Awards in 2024. Boygenius announced a hiatus in February 2024.

In the period since, Bridgers stepped back from public-facing activity. Lost Boys is her documented return. The album is released on Dead Oceans, the Bloomington, Indiana-based independent label that has been her recorded home since her 2017 debut, Stranger in the Alps.
