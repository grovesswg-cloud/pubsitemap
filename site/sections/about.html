We Do Not
Cover Music.
We Crown It.

LORD is an independent online music publication built for the era of cultural collapse and sonic sovereignty. We are not a playlist. We are not an algorithm. We are not a hot take.

LORD exists at the intersection of music criticism, cultural archaeology, and artist portraiture. We publish features that take their time, reviews that have a point of view, and conversations that treat the artist as a thinking being — not content.

Our voice is authoritative without being cold. Reverent without being sycophantic. We write for the reader who listens before they judge, and judges with intelligence.

Every issue, every article, every word published under the LORD name must pass one test: does it add something to the record? If not, it does not run.

The Anatomy of LORD
The Feature
Long-Form Artist Portraits
3,000–8,000 words. Photography by LORD-commissioned photographers. Published monthly. Every Feature must propose a thesis about the artist’s work and place in the culture.
The Review
Records on a Five-Point Scale
Not stars. A word: Dormant / Rising / Sovereign / Eternal / Monument. 800–1,200 words. Published within 72 hours of release. LORD does not review artists who advertise with us.
The Interview
Conversation in Full
Published complete — no selective editing for narrative convenience. The interviewer is never the focus. Minimum one-hour session. Q&A, not paraphrase.
The Sermon
One Writer. One Argument.
600–1,000 words. The Sermon is always signed. LORD does not publish unsigned opinion. Disagreement with a Sermon is welcome and published alongside it.
The Archive
History Is Living
Historical criticism. Reassessment of records ten years or older. LORD believes the archive is living. We revisit, revise, and reclaim what was missed or misread.
The Bulletin
News. Short. Factual.
Never speculative. Linked to source. The Bulletin does not editorialize. If LORD has an opinion on the news, that belongs in The Sermon. Published daily, maximum three.
Editorial Standards

The LORD Standard

LORD is not affiliated with any record label, streaming platform, or music industry body. We accept no advertorial content. Every review subject is editorially selected — never commercially placed.

Our photography policy: every image published under the LORD name is either commissioned directly or licensed from the photographer. We do not use stock photography, AI-generated images, or paparazzi candids. The visual record must be as rigorous as the written one.

LORD publishes corrections openly. If we get something wrong, we say so — in the same placement as the original error, with full transparency about what changed and why.
