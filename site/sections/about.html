We Do Not
Cover Music.
We Crown It.

LORD is an independent online music publication built for the era of cultural collapse and sonic sovereignty. We are not a playlist. We are not an algorithm. We are not a hot take.

LORD exists at the intersection of music criticism, cultural archaeology, and artist portraiture. We publish features that take their time, reviews that have a point of view, and dispatches that treat music news with the seriousness it deserves.

Our voice is authoritative without being cold. Reverent without being sycophantic. We write for the reader who listens before they judge, and judges with intelligence.

Every article, every word published under the LORD name must pass one test: does it add something to the record? If not, it does not run.

What LORD Publishes
The Feature
Long-Form Editorial. Daily.
One feature every day. 1,500–2,000 words. Every Feature proposes a thesis about the artist’s work and cultural significance. This is not news. It is argument.
The Review
Records on a Five-Point Scale
Two current album reviews per week — the most significant releases of the moment. Plus one historical reassessment each month: a classic album, re-examined with the benefit of time. Rating: Dormant / Rising / Sovereign / Eternal / Monument. No stars. A verdict.
The Sermon
One Writer. One Argument.
600–1,000 words. The Sermon is always signed. LORD does not publish unsigned opinion. Disagreement with a Sermon is welcome and published alongside it.
The Bulletin
News. Short. Factual.
Up to four bulletins per day. Never speculative. Always linked to source. The Bulletin does not editorialize. If LORD has an opinion on the news, that belongs in The Sermon.
Editorial Standards

The LORD Standard

LORD is not affiliated with any record label, streaming platform, or music industry body. We accept no advertorial content. Every review subject is editorially selected — never commercially placed.

Our Reviews track every album that passes through this publication. No album is reviewed twice. The historical record is permanent.

LORD publishes corrections openly. If we get something wrong, we say so — in the same placement as the original error, with full transparency about what changed and why.
